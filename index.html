Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the questions we hear most from homeowners planning a project in Sonoma County.

Visit CSLB.ca.gov and look up the contractor's license number. Confirm it's active, the classification is Class B (General Building), and the bond and insurance are current. Always ask for the license number before signing any contract — a legitimate contractor provides it immediately without hesitation.
Yes — get 2 to 3 detailed bids. But don't automatically choose the lowest. A low bid often means inferior materials, unlicensed subs, or gaps in scope that appear as change orders once work starts. Compare bids line by line. Ask each contractor what's not included. The best bid is detailed, realistic, and comes from a contractor with verifiable references.
Ask: (1) What is your CA license number? (2) Who manages the job site daily? (3) How do you handle change orders? (4) What is your payment schedule? (5) Can you provide 3 references from similar projects in the last 2 years? (6) Are all subcontractors licensed and insured? Any hesitation on these is a red flag.
A mid-range kitchen remodel typically runs $40,000–$90,000. High-end custom kitchens with structural changes can reach $120,000–$200,000+. Key cost drivers: whether you're moving plumbing or electrical, cabinet quality (stock vs semi-custom vs full custom), countertop material, and overall square footage. We provide line-item estimates so you see exactly where every dollar goes.
In Sonoma County, home additions typically cost $300–$550 per square foot for standard construction, and $600–$900+ for high-end finishes or complex structural work. A 600 sq ft ADU might run $180,000–$350,000 all-in depending on the site and utility requirements.
The industry standard is 10–20% above your quoted contract price. Older homes (pre-1970s) are more likely to reveal hidden issues once walls are opened — dry rot, outdated wiring, or plumbing that needs upgrading. We communicate all discoveries immediately and never proceed with additional work without your written authorization.
A complete fiberglass pool installation in Sonoma County typically runs $65,000–$110,000 including excavation, shell, plumbing, electrical, decking, and equipment. Gunite pools run higher ($90,000–$150,000+). Fiberglass pools are lower maintenance long-term — no replastering, smoother surface, faster install. We are an authorized Empire Fiberglass Pools dealer.
Cosmetic work (replacing fixtures, tile, or vanity in the same location) typically does not require a permit. Structural or system changes do — including moving walls, relocating plumbing, adding an electrical circuit, or changing the layout. Unpermitted work in California can affect your insurance, create problems at resale, and result in fines.
A home addition requires a Building Permit plus separate permits for Electrical, Plumbing, and Mechanical (HVAC). For larger additions, Planning Department review may also be required for zoning compliance. We have established relationships with the City of Santa Rosa and Sonoma County permit offices and handle the entire submission and follow-up process on your behalf.
Unpermitted work must be disclosed to buyers in California and can significantly complicate a sale. Buyers may demand the work be brought up to code at your expense, lenders may refuse to finance the property, and insurance claims related to unpermitted work can be denied. Always permit and inspect construction properly.
A standard kitchen remodel (no structural changes) takes 4–8 weeks from demo to final punch list. Complex remodels with structural modifications or custom cabinetry can extend to 10–14 weeks. The biggest variable is cabinet lead time — semi-custom: 3–6 weeks; full custom: 8–14 weeks. We pre-order all long-lead materials before demo begins.

California law limits the initial deposit to 10% of the contract price or $1,000, whichever is less for home improvement contracts. After that, payments are tied to project milestones — never paid ahead of work completed. Final payment is only due after your written sign-off on the completed punch list.
All Howard Construction projects include a 1-year workmanship warranty covering defects in labor and installation. California law also provides a 10-year structural defect warranty on new construction and major structural work. We provide all warranty documentation at project completion and remain accessible for any post-project follow-up.
