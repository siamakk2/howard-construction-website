FAQ
Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the questions we hear most from homeowners planning a project across Northern California.

Visit CSLB.ca.gov and look up the license number. Confirm it is active, Class B, and insurance is current. A legitimate contractor provides it immediately without hesitation.
Yes — get 2 to 3 detailed bids. Never automatically choose the lowest. A low bid often means inferior materials or gaps in scope that appear as change orders later. Compare line by line and ask what is NOT included.
(1) What is your CA license number? (2) Who manages the job site daily? (3) How do you handle change orders? (4) What is your payment schedule? (5) Can you provide 3 references from the last 2 years? (6) Are all subs licensed and insured?
Mid-range typically runs $40,000–$90,000. High-end custom kitchens reach $120,000–$200,000+. Key drivers: cabinet quality, plumbing/electrical moves, countertop material. We provide line-item estimates so every dollar is accounted for.
In Northern California, additions typically cost $300–$550 per sq ft for standard work, $600–$900+ for high-end finishes. A 600 sq ft ADU might run $180,000–$350,000 all-in.
The industry standard is 10–20% above the quoted price. Older homes often reveal hidden issues once walls open — dry rot, outdated wiring, plumbing. We communicate all discoveries immediately and never proceed without your written authorization.
Cosmetic work typically does not need a permit. Structural or system changes do — moving walls, relocating plumbing, adding circuits, changing layout. Unpermitted work in California can affect insurance and create problems at resale.
A home addition requires Building, Electrical, Plumbing, and Mechanical permits. We have established relationships with permit offices across Northern California — Santa Rosa, Napa, Fairfield, and beyond — and handle the entire submission and follow-up for you.
A standard kitchen (no structural changes) takes 4–8 weeks from demo to punch list. Complex remodels extend to 10–14 weeks. We pre-order all long-lead materials before demo begins so nothing waits on parts.

All projects include a 1-year workmanship warranty. California law also provides a 10-year structural defect warranty on new construction and major structural work. All documentation provided at project completion.
