FAQ
Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the questions we hear most from homeowners planning a project across Northern California.

Visit CSLB.ca.gov and look up the contractor's license number. Confirm it is active, the classification is Class B (General Building), and the bond and insurance are current. Always ask for the license number before signing any contract — a legitimate contractor provides it immediately.
Yes — get 2 to 3 detailed bids. But never automatically choose the lowest. A low bid often means inferior materials, unlicensed subs, or gaps in scope that show up as change orders once work starts. Compare bids line by line and ask each contractor what is NOT included.
Ask: (1) What is your CA license number? (2) Who manages the job site daily? (3) How do you handle change orders? (4) What is your payment schedule? (5) Can you provide 3 references from similar projects in the last 2 years? (6) Are all subcontractors licensed and insured?
A mid-range kitchen remodel in Sonoma or Napa County typically runs $40,000–$90,000. High-end custom kitchens with structural changes can reach $120,000–$200,000+. Key cost drivers: cabinet quality, whether you are moving plumbing or electrical, and countertop material. We provide line-item estimates so you see exactly where every dollar goes.
In Northern California, home additions typically cost $300–$550 per square foot for standard construction, and $600–$900+ for high-end finishes. A 600 sq ft ADU might run $180,000–$350,000 all-in depending on site and utility requirements.
The industry standard is 10–20% above your quoted contract price. Older homes are more likely to reveal hidden issues once walls are opened — dry rot, outdated wiring, or plumbing. We communicate all discoveries immediately and never proceed without your written authorization.
A complete fiberglass pool installation in Northern California typically runs $65,000–$110,000 including excavation, shell, plumbing, electrical, and decking. Gunite pools run higher ($90,000–$150,000+). Fiberglass pools are lower maintenance long-term — no replastering, smoother surface, faster installation.
Cosmetic work (replacing fixtures, tile, or vanity in the same location) typically does not require a permit. Structural or system changes do — including moving walls, relocating plumbing, adding an electrical circuit, or changing the layout. Unpermitted work in California can affect your insurance and create problems at resale.
A home addition requires a Building Permit plus separate permits for Electrical, Plumbing, and Mechanical (HVAC). We have established relationships with permit offices in Santa Rosa, Napa, Fairfield, and all Northern California counties we serve. We handle the entire submission and follow-up process on your behalf.
A standard kitchen remodel (no structural changes) takes 4–8 weeks from demo to final punch list. Complex remodels with structural modifications or custom cabinetry can extend to 10–14 weeks. We pre-order all long-lead materials before demo begins so your kitchen is not left open waiting on parts.

We use daily dust barriers, schedule noisy work during hours you specify, perform nightly site cleanup, and maintain access to essential areas. We have completed numerous full remodels with families living on-site throughout the entire construction period.
Philip Howard personally oversees every Howard Construction project. Our lead foreman Rich is on-site daily for larger projects, with Phil visiting multiple times per week. You will always have Philip's direct cell number — no call center, no office buffer. If you have a question, you reach the decision-maker directly.
We stop work, document the issue with photos, and contact you immediately. We explain the options and costs and do not proceed until you have authorized any additional scope in writing. We never surprise clients with change order costs at the end of a project. Transparency is non-negotiable.
California law limits the initial deposit to 10% of the contract price or $1,000, whichever is less for home improvement contracts. After that, payments are tied to project milestones — never paid ahead of work completed. Final payment is only due after your written sign-off on the completed punch list.
All Howard Construction projects include a 1-year workmanship warranty covering defects in labor and installation. California law also provides a 10-year structural defect warranty on new construction and major structural work. We provide all warranty documentation at project completion.
Every subcontractor we use is licensed, bonded, and insured in California. We work with a trusted network of plumbers, electricians, tile setters, and other specialists — many of whom we have worked with for 10+ years. We verify their CSLB license status before every project.
